# Credit Reports

## What is a Credit Report?
A credit report is a detailed record of your entire credit history, maintained by credit bureaus. While a credit score is a single number summarising your creditworthiness, a credit report is the full story behind that number.

Think of it this way: the credit score is the grade, the credit report is the answer sheet.

## What a Credit Report Contains

### 1. Personal Information
- Name, date of birth, PAN number, Aadhaar (masked), addresses, phone numbers
- This information is sourced from loan and credit card applications you've submitted to lenders over the years
- Errors here (wrong PAN, wrong date of birth) can cause your report to be mixed up with someone else's — always verify

### 2. Account Information (Credit Summary)
The core of your report. Lists every credit account you have or have had:
- **Account type:** Credit card, personal loan, home loan, etc.
- **Lender name:** Which bank or NBFC issued it
- **Account number** (partially masked)
- **Date opened**
- **Credit limit or loan amount**
- **Current outstanding balance**
- **Account status:** Active, Closed, Written Off, Settled
- **Payment history:** Month-by-month record of whether you paid on time (shown as DPD — Days Past Due)

### 3. Enquiry Information
Every time a lender pulled your credit report (hard enquiry), it is recorded here with:
- Name of the lender
- Date of enquiry
- Type of credit applied for (loan, credit card, etc.)

Multiple enquiries in a short period are visible to all lenders reviewing your report.

### 4. Public Records (if any)
Court judgments, bankruptcies, or debt recovery tribunal (DRT) orders. Rare for most individuals but extremely damaging if present.

## How to Read Your DPD (Days Past Due) History
The payment history section shows a grid — one row per account, one column per month. Each cell shows:
- **000 or STD:** Standard — paid on time
- **30:** Payment was 30 days late
- **60:** Payment was 60 days late
- **90:** Payment was 90 days late — NPA territory
- **SUB / DBT / LSS:** Substandard, Doubtful, Loss — progressively worse NPA categories
- **SMA:** Special Mention Account — early warning stage before NPA

A clean report shows STD or 000 across all accounts for all months.

## Your Right to a Free Credit Report
Under RBI regulations, every Indian citizen is entitled to **one free credit report per year from each bureau.**

- CIBIL: www.cibil.com
- Experian: www.experian.in
- CRIF High Mark: www.crifhighmark.com
- Equifax: www.equifax.co.in

Since you have four bureaus, you can get four free reports per year. A practical strategy: check one bureau every quarter to monitor your credit health throughout the year.

You can also check your score for free anytime on apps like Bachatt, CRED, BankBazaar, Paisabazaar, or OneScore.

## How Long Information Stays on Your Report
Under CICRA 2005 and RBI guidelines, credit information is retained for **7 years from the date of last activity** on the account. This applies to:
- Settled accounts
- Written-off accounts
- NPA accounts
- Closed accounts (positive history)
- All enquiries (though enquiries are typically given less weight after 1–2 years)

After 7 years, negative information ages off your report automatically.

## How to Dispute Errors on Your Credit Report
Errors on credit reports are more common than people realise. Common errors include:
- A loan you never took (could be identity fraud or a data error)
- An account marked "Active" that you closed years ago
- Payments marked as missed when you paid on time
- Wrong personal details (name spelling, PAN, address)
- Duplicate accounts listed twice

**Steps to raise a dispute:**

1. **Identify the error** — note the account, the lender, and exactly what is wrong
2. **Go to the bureau's website** — each bureau has an online dispute portal
3. **Submit the dispute** — provide your details, specify the error, and attach supporting documents (bank statement, closure letter, etc.)
4. **Bureau contacts the lender** — the bureau is legally required to verify the information with the lender
5. **Resolution within 30 days** — per RBI guidelines, disputes must be resolved within 30 days

If the lender confirms it was their error, the bureau updates your report. If the lender disputes your claim, you can escalate to the RBI Ombudsman.

**Important:** You cannot remove accurate negative information from your report. Only errors can be disputed. Anyone promising to "clean" accurate negative history from your report is running a scam.

## Credit Report vs Credit Score — Key Differences
| | Credit Report | Credit Score |
|---|---|---|
| What it is | Full detailed history | Single 3-digit summary |
| Updated | Monthly (as lenders report) | Monthly (recalculated) |
| Length | Can be many pages | One number (300–900) |
| Free access | Once per year per bureau | Many platforms offer free checks anytime |
| Used for | Deep due diligence by lenders | Quick initial screening |

## Monitoring Your Credit Report — Best Practices
- Check your full report at least once a year
- Check your score more frequently (quarterly) — sudden drops signal something has changed
- Set up alerts on apps like CRED or OneScore that notify you when your report changes
- If you suspect fraud (unknown accounts or enquiries), freeze your credit by contacting the bureau directly
